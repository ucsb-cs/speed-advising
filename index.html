Come to the Computer Engineering and Computer Science Department's Faculty-Undergraduate Speed Advising event. This fun and informational event is offered regularly for CE and CS majors.

October 25th, 2pm - 6pm at HFH 1132

These faculty members are available during (part of) this time period. (PDF version) 

The semi-formal title of the event, "Speed Advising," was picked by the student members of the CS Undergraduate Affairs Committee to indicate the flexible 'first-available matchup' format for the meetings between undergraduates and the CE and CS faculty. 

Drop in for a 20 minutes info session with faculty about:

  • Research opportunities within the CS department and CE program
  • Internship & job advice
  • Major Field Electives
  • Graduate school advice
  • General networking

On Wednesday, begin by coming to Advising Central (1132 Harold Frank Hall) between 2pm and 6pm to sign up for a slot, have a slice of pizza, and hang out. There will be CS staff and students there to answer general questions and help you choose a faculty slot.

This hopefully provides some positive interactions and  information exchanges between students and faculty members, including a better overview of the research going on in the department, as well as pointers to opportunities, and also satisfies a degree requirement for CS Majors (by getting informed about Major Electives).

We hope you will strongly consider attending and getting your questions answered in this low-pressure environment!
